### Evaluation Statement

This is a thoughtful, disciplined conceptual architecture project that treats a high-security facility as an integrated **trust architecture** rather than a checklist of isolated controls (badges, doors, cameras, etc.). It emphasizes contextual trust, movement as a signal, zone differentiation, post-access control, privileged access separation, degraded operations, recovery as trust restoration, and compatibility with life safety.

The work stands out for its systems-thinking rigor, rejection of binary trust models, and focus on what happens *after* initial access decisions. The whitepaper layer and structured docs enhance navigability and external readability. It is clearly a conceptual/maturating framework—not an engineering blueprint or product—and reads as professionally credible for architectural thinking, threat modeling, and discussion.

Strengths include strong internal coherence, distinctive synthesis (zone + movement + trust-state logic), and handling of "messy" realities like degradation, exceptions, and human interpretability. Limitations are natural for this stage: limited applied examples/workflows in some areas, conceptual rather than quantified/validated, and heavy on principles (which is appropriate).
